Visualizing Time
In this project, I explored the idea of how we can create a clock without numerical elements. I used basic shapes to translate time and created appealing visual with interactive elements.
Main tools are P5.js and CSS, I also added ‘Tweakpane’ as an optional tool for users to play with design elements.

Air Quality in New York City
The purpose of this design is to help us understand the big picture and see where the most vulnerable areas are, impacted by PM 2.5. Using heatmap will also spark more curiosities on how we can use this base insights to investigate more findings and eventually drive changes in the policy level.
I used mainly leaflet.JS to create the map and a liittle bit of D3.js to visualize numbers as a bar graph.

Visualizing Earthquake
An earthquake is a violent and abrupt shaking of the ground, caused by movement between tectonic plates along a fault line in the earth's crust. Earthquakes can result in the ground shaking, soil liquefaction, landslides, fissures, avalanches, fires and tsunamis. The extent of destruction and harm caused by an earthquake depends on multiple factors i.e. magnitude, intensity and duration, etc. In the following visualization, we will give you a glimpse of the most 'important' earthquakes that happened in the past month / last 30 days.
Using the data set of the significant earthquakes happened in the past month which I extracted from USGS, visualizing with leaflet.js and d3.js
